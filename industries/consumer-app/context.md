# Industry Context: Consumer App

## Domain Characteristics
- Retention is the primary challenge (not acquisition)
- First session = make or break for long-term engagement
- Competing for attention against every other app on the phone
- Habit formation is the design goal for daily-use apps

## Key User Mental Models
- "What's in it for me, right now?" — value must be immediate
- Social validation matters (what are my friends doing / using)
- Notifications are a double-edged sword — too many = uninstall
- Privacy expectations are rising (especially Gen Z)
- "I want control without having to configure everything"

## Common UX Anti-Patterns (avoid)
- Long onboarding before showing core value
- Mandatory sign-up before try (kills conversion)
- Notification prompts too early (before user sees value)
- Rating prompts immediately after download
- No guest/anonymous mode for low-commitment entry
- Unclear data usage / permissions requests

## High-Value UX Patterns
- Immediate value demonstration in first 60 seconds
- Progressive onboarding (collect info contextually, not upfront)
- Variable reward loops for engagement-driven apps
- Personalization that shows results quickly
- Social proof woven into the experience
- Offboarding that re-engages (not just confirms delete)
- Offline-first capability where relevant

## Key Metrics for Consumer App UX
- D1 / D7 / D30 retention rates
- Onboarding completion rate
- Feature adoption rate (breadth of use)
- Session frequency and length
- Notification opt-in rate
- App store rating and review sentiment

## Competitive Reference Points
- Duolingo: Habit-forming gamification
- Spotify: Personalization and discovery patterns
- Instagram: Social engagement loops
- Swiggy / Zomato: Local consumer app patterns (India)
- CRED: Premium consumer UX for niche audience (India)
- WhatsApp: Simplicity at massive scale
