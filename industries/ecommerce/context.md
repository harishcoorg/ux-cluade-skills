# Industry Context: E-Commerce

## Domain Characteristics
- Conversion is the primary business metric — every friction point costs revenue
- Discovery, comparison, and confidence are the three pillars of purchase decision
- Returns and post-purchase experience are as important as pre-purchase
- Mobile-first: 70%+ of browsing is on mobile in most markets

## Key User Mental Models
- "Is this trustworthy?" — verified reviews, recognizable payment methods, return policy visibility
- "Am I getting a good deal?" — price anchoring, comparisons, urgency cues
- "Will it fit / look right?" — size guides, real photos, AR try-on
- "When will I get it?" — delivery transparency is a purchase trigger
- Abandoned cart = decision not made yet, not rejection

## Common UX Anti-Patterns (avoid)
- Forced account creation before checkout (guest checkout is mandatory)
- Hiding total cost (shipping surprise at checkout = #1 abandon reason)
- Fake urgency ("Only 2 left!" when stock is plentiful)
- Cluttered category pages with no filtering
- Non-persistent cart across sessions/devices
- Captcha on checkout

## High-Value UX Patterns
- Sticky add-to-cart CTA while scrolling product page
- Persistent cart with item count in header
- One-page / streamlined checkout (max 3 steps)
- "Frequently bought with" social proof on product page
- Real-time stock indicator (not just "in stock")
- Address autocomplete (reduces checkout drop-off significantly)
- Wishlist as consideration layer

## Key Metrics for E-Commerce UX
- Conversion rate (add-to-cart → purchase)
- Cart abandonment rate
- Checkout completion rate
- Return rate (indicator of expectation mismatch)
- Search exit rate (no results or bad results)
- Mobile vs. desktop conversion gap

## Competitive Reference Points
- Amazon: Search-first discovery, extreme efficiency
- Zara / ASOS: Visual-first fashion browsing
- Flipkart / Meesho: Value-segment UX patterns (India)
- Shopify stores: Small-merchant conversion patterns
- Nykaa / Myntra: Category-specific browsing patterns (India)
